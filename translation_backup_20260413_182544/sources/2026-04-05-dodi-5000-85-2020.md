## Key Finding: MOSA Legal Requirements (Page 26, §3C.3.a.(5))

**This is the definitive MOSA policy section.** The previous ingest of DoDI 5000.02 found ZERO MOSA mentions — but this is where the actual requirements live.

> "Pursuant to **Section 2446a of Title 10, U.S.C.**, PMs are responsible for **evaluating and implementing MOSA to the maximum extent feasible and cost effective.**"

### Three Pillars Required:

1. **Architecture modularity**
2. **Open interfaces** (widely supported, consensus-based standards)
3. **Appropriate business practices**

### Acquisition Strategy Must Identify (§3C.3.a.(5)(a)):

- **Where, why, and how** MOSA will be used
- **MDAPs receiving Milestone A or B after Jan 1, 2019** must be designed and developed with MOSA to the maximum extent practicable

### For MDAPs using MOSA, the acquisition strategy must clearly describe (§3C.3.a.(5)(b)):

a. **How MOSA will be used**, including business and technical considerations

b. **Differentiation** between:
   - Major system platform
   - Major system components developed under the program
   - Major system components developed outside the program

c. **Evolution of capabilities** — what will be added, removed, or replaced in future increments

d. **Additional major system components** that may be added later

e. **IP and data rights** — including technical data deliverables

f. **System integration and configuration management** for cyber threat environment

g. **Technical Data Package (TDP)** — sufficient detail to enable competitive procurement

### Competition (§3C.3.a.(4)):

DoD will use **competitive prototyping, dual sourcing, and MOSA** to enable competition for upgrades and subsystem-level competition.

### RFP Requirement (§3C.3.a.(5)(b)):

> "The MDA for an MDAP that uses MOSA must ensure that the RFPs for the EMD phase and the P&D phase **describe the MOSA and the minimum set of major system components** that must be included in the system design."

## Acquisition Pathway Overview — Major Capability

This document covers the **Major Capability Acquisition pathway** under AAF. Key milestones:

MDD → MSA Phase → Milestone A → TMRR Phase → Dev RFP Release → Milestone B → EMD Phase → Milestone C → P&D Phase → FRP/FD Decision → O&S Phase

### Decision Points:
- **MDD** (Materiel Development Decision): Entry point, requires AoA
- **Milestone A**: Technology maturity, risk reduction
- **Milestone B**: Engineering & Manufacturing Development start
- **Milestone C**: Production decision
- **FRP** (Full-Rate Production): Commit to production quantities

## APB (Acquisition Program Baseline)

PM develops APB, documents cost/schedule/performance baselines. **Binding agreement** between MDA, CAE, PEO, and PM. KPPs from validated CDD listed verbatim in APB.

## Options Matrix (Figure 3)

MDA provides options matrix to Joint Staff, USD(A&S), USD(R&E), DCAPE after AoA completion covering:
- Technical feasibility
- Cost estimates
- Schedule alternatives
- Affordability analysis
- Risk aggregation

## International Acquisition (3C.4)

MDAPs must include exportability considerations, with a **Security and Exportability Review** at each milestone.

## Industrial Base Analysis

PM must conduct **industrial base assessment** to identify supply chain risks, single-point-of-failure, and domestic/foreign sourcing concerns.

## Significance for MOSA Research

This is the **single most important legal document** for MOSA. It proves:

1. **MOSA is legally mandated** for MDAPs post-2019 (Title 10, USC 2446a), not optional
2. **Specific required elements** defined (architecture, interfaces, business practices)
3. **IP and data rights** explicitly required in acquisition strategy
4. **Future increment evolution planning** must be documented from the start
5. **Competition** at subsystem level is the primary MOSA goal

The document doesn't specify **how** to evaluate whether MOSA is "feasible and cost effective" — which aligns with Shah's H1 finding of no standardized metrics.
