## Text Content
The Modular Open Systems Approach requirement for major defense acquisition programs is codified in 10 U.S.C. § 4401:

"Requirements for MOSA in major defense acquisition programs; definitions."

(a) In General.—The Secretary of Defense shall require, to the maximum extent practicable, that each major defense acquisition program that is planned to receive Milestone A or Milestone B approval after December 31, 2018, be designed and developed using a modular open systems approach.

(b) Definitions.—In this section:
(1) The term “major defense acquisition program” means a major defense acquisition program as defined in section 2430 of this title.
(2) The term “modular open system” means a system that is designed in accordance with this section.
(3) The term “modular system interface” means an interface that—
(A) is defined in accordance with widely known or publicly available standards;
(B) is provided with machine-readable documentation;
(C) is sufficient to enable competition;
(D) is sufficient to enable interoperability; and
(E) is sufficient to support technology refresh.

(c) Construction.—Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit the authority of the Secretary of Defense to—
(1) require a major defense acquisition program to be designed and developed using a modular open systems approach; or
(2) prohibit a major defense acquisition program from being designed and developed using a modular open systems approach.

## Key Provisions
- **Applicability**: Major defense acquisition programs receiving Milestone A or B approval after Dec 31, 2018
- **Requirement**: Must be designed and developed using MOSA "to the maximum extent practicable"
- **Definitions**: Clearly defines MOSA, modular system, and modular system interface
- **Construction Clause**: Does not limit or prohibit MOSA use - it is a requirement
