It covers missile system description, guidance, control, propulsion, aerodynamics, and structural considerations for missile flight simulation.\n\n## Key Points\n- **Purpose**: Guidance only - not to be cited as a requirement for contractors\n- **Scope**: Surface-to-air missile flight simulation from launch to target engagement\n- **Simulation Approach**: Based on mathematical models of missile, target, and environment\n- **Key Subsystems Covered**:\n  - Guidance systems (command, line-of-sight, beam rider, proportional navigation, optimal)\n  - Autopilot and control (lateral acceleration, canard/tail/wing control, servomotors)\n  - Warhead and fuze (shaped charge, continuous rod, fragment warheads)\n  - Propulsion (boost-glide, boost-sustain, specific impulse, temperature effects)\n  - Airframe (typical configurations, static stability, rolling considerations)\n  - Aerodynamics (coefficients, force/moment equations, atmospheric properties)\n- **Computational Cycle**: Describes the iterative process of missile flight simulation\n- **Coordinate Systems**: Essential for simulating missile and target motion\n- **Distribution Statement A**: Approved for public release; distribution unlimited\n\n## Related\n- [[mosa-defense-acquisition]] - MOSA在国防采购中的应用\n- [[wosa-architecture-tools]] - WOSA架构工具链要求\n- [[technical-document-75195d4e]] - 原始技术文档处理记录\n- [[face-technical-standard]] - Face技术标准\n- [[adaptive-acquisition-framework]] - 自适应获取框架\n\n## Notes\n> This handbook represents foundational guidance for missile flight simulation engineering.
