# 10 U.S.C. §§4401–4403 — MOSA Statute

## Document Meta
- **§4401**: Requirement for MOSA in MDAPs (originally §2446a)
- **§4402**: Requirement to address MOSA in capabilities development (originally §2446b)
- **§4403**: Requirements relating to availability of major system interfaces (originally §2446c)
- **Total pages**: 16 (3 PDFs: §4401=7p, §4402=5p, §4403=4p)
- **MOSA mentions**: 22 across all 3 sections

## §4401 — The Mandate

> "A major defense acquisition program that receives Milestone A or Milestone B approval after January 1, 2019, shall be designed and developed, to the maximum extent practicable, with a modular open system approach to enable incremental development and enhance competition, innovation, and interoperability."

**Key definition**: MOSA = integrated business and technical strategy that:
1. Employs modular design with modular system interfaces
2. Interfaces comply with widely supported consensus-based standards
3. Facilitates incremental development, interoperability, competition

**Additional requirement**: DoD shall make MOSA implementation standards publicly available (unless service acquisition executive submits request to not disclose and Secretary approves).

## §4402 — Capabilities Development

Program capability documents must identify:
1. Expected evolution of system performance requirements during lifecycle
2. Minimum acceptable capability for initial operating capability

Analysis of Alternatives (AoA) must include consideration of:
- Evolutionary acquisition
- Prototyping
- **Modular open system approach**

Acquisition strategy must:
1. Clearly describe MOSA to be used
2. Differentiate platform vs components
3. Describe anticipated component additions/removals/replacements
4. Identify additional components for later lifecycle
5. Describe IP and technical data deliverables
6. Define major system interfaces and how they will remain available

**Reporting**: If program does NOT use MOSA, must certify that MOSA is not practicable.

## §4403 — Major System Interfaces

Each military department secretary SHALL:
1. Coordinate with other services, agencies, private sector, standards orgs
2. Ensure major system interfaces incorporate **commercial standards and consensus-based standards** to maximum extent practicable
3. Ensure sufficient systems engineering expertise and resources for MOSA
4. Ensure PPBE resources for MOSA specification, identification, development, sustainment
5. **Provide adequate MOSA training to requirements and acquisition workforce**
6. Issue guidance to implement requirements

## Key Observation

**"To the maximum extent practicable"** — this phrase appears in all 3 sections. It creates a **presumptive requirement** (MOSA must be used) with an **escape clause** (unless not practicable). This is the tension that Shah's thesis explores and that the MOSA Implementation Guidebook addresses.
