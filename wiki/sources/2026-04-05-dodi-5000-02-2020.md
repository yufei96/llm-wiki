# DoDI 5000.02 Summary (2020, Change 1 2022)

## Document Meta

- **Effective**: January 23, 2020; Change 1: June 8, 2022
- **Originator**: USD(A&S) — Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment
- **Replaces**: January 7, 2015 version of DoDI 5000.02 (cancelled and renumbered as 5000.02T, now also cancelled)

## Key Insight: MOSA Absent

**This document does NOT mention MOSA at all.** The 2020 version was restructured from the original 2015 edition. The detailed MOSA-related content (formerly in Enclosures 1–13 of the 2015 version) was migrated to separate DoD Instructions.

## AAF — Adaptive Acquisition Framework

**Purpose**: Deliver effective, suitable, survivable, sustainable, and affordable solutions to the end user in a timely manner through **6 pathways**.

## 6 Acquisition Pathways

### 1. Urgent Capability Acquisition
- **Goal**: Field capabilities in **< 2 years**
- **Cost cap**: $525M RDT&E or $3.065B procurement (FY2020 constant $)
- **Timeline**: Planning in weeks, development/production in months
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.81

### 2. Middle Tier of Acquisition (MTA)
- **Purpose**: Rapid prototyping (field within **5 years**) or rapid fielding (start production within **6 months**, complete within **5 years**)
- **Exemptions**: Not subject to JCIDS or DoDD 5000.01 procedures
- **Note**: USD(A&S) approval required if program exceeds MDAP threshold
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.80

### 3. Major Capability Acquisition
- **Purpose**: Enduring military-unique capability
- **Structure**: Analyze → Design → Develop → Integrate → Test → Evaluate → Produce → Support
- **MDAP-level**: USD(A&S) serves as MDA for ACAT ID programs
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.85

### 4. Software Acquisition
- **Purpose**: Agile software development
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.87

### 5. Defense Business Systems (DBS) Acquisition
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.84

### 6. Acquisition of Services
- **Reference**: DoDI 5000.74

## Decision Hierarchy

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **MDA/DA** (Milestone Decision Authority) | Program decision authority; specifies decision points and procedures |
| **PEO** (Program Executive Officer) | Portfolio risk, cost, schedule, performance, interoperability |
| **PM** (Program Manager) | Plan, prepare, execute acquisition and product support strategies |
| **Product Support Manager** | Comprehensive product support strategy under PM direction |

## Key Responsibilities

- **USD(A&S)**: Serves as MDA for ACAT ID programs (Materiel Development Decision, Milestone A/B/C)
- **USD(R&E)**: Conducts independent technical risk assessments (ITRAs) for ACAT ID; confirms technical feasibility
- **CAE** (Component Acquisition Executive): Implements direction, may submit waivers

## General Procedures (4.1)

- **Tailoring-in**: PMs identify and recommend regulatory information for MDA approval — customize documentation to program
- **Multi-pathway**: PMs may combine pathways; must define transition points and coordinate entry requirements
- **Cybersecurity**: Must be addressed early and continuously in program planning
- **Data rights**: Consider procurement of data deliverables and license rights for competitive acquisition
- **Product support & affordability**: Prioritize during early program planning
- **Risk management**: PM must establish program; determine which risks to mitigate vs. accept

## Implications for MOSA Research

The thesis (Shah 2021) cited DoDI 5000.02 as the MOSA mandate source. But the actual MOSA directive is in **Better Buying Power 3.0** and **USC 10 §2446a**. DoDI 5000.02 is the **process framework** (AAF) that programs must follow, not the MOSA policy itself. The 2015 version may have referenced MOSA, but the 2020 version is a restructured framework document. **MOSA-specific implementation guidance is likely in DoDI 5000.85 (Major Capability Acquisition) or separate USD(A&S) memoranda.**
