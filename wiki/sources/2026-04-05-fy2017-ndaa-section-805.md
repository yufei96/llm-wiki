## Section 805 — The MOSA Legislative Origin

**This is where MOSA law originated.** Section 805 of the FY2017 NDAA added Chapter 144B to Title 10 USC, creating:

- **§2446a** — Requirement for modular open system approach in development of major weapon systems
- **§2446b** — Requirement to address MOSA in program capabilities development and acquisition weapon system design
- **§2446c** — Requirements relating to availability of major system interfaces

These were later renumbered to **10 USC §4401-4403** (2021 renumbering).

### Key Provisions from Section 805

**(a) Modular Open System Approach**
> "Part IV of subtitle A of title 10, United States Code, is amended by inserting after chapter 144A the following new chapter: Chapter 144B—Weapon Systems Development and Related Matters"

This amendment created the legal structure that still governs MOSA today.

**(b) Section 2446a Definition** (§805(a)(1))
> "The term 'modular open system approach' means, with respect to a major defense acquisition program, an integrated business and technical strategy that—"(1) employs a modular design (2) is subjected to verification that modular system interfaces comply with widely supported, consensus-based standards

**(c) Reporting Requirements** (§805(2))
Programs must report to Congress:
- Whether MOSA was used (Yes/No)
- If not, justification for why MOSA was not practicable
- Brief summary of MOSA key elements

**(d) Data Rights** (§805(3))
For technical data developed exclusively at private expense and used in a modular open system approach — addresses intellectual property considerations.

## Section 809 — Technical Data Rights Amendments
Related to §805: amendments to 10 USC 2320 (later renumbered to §3771) regarding technical data deliverables and intellectual property — directly supporting MOSA's open interface requirements.

## Historical Context

1. **FY2016**: HASC Report 114-102 — "not sufficiently agile to support warfighter demands"
2. **FY2017 NDAA §805**: First codified MOSA into law (Dec 2016)
3. **FY2021 NDAA §804**: Expanded to ALL defense acquisition programs (Jan 2021)
4. **DoDI 5000.85**: Implemented via policy (2020, Chg1 2021)

## Significance

This NDAA is the **birth certificate of MOSA law**. Every subsequent MOSA policy, guidance, and practice flows from Section 805. Understanding this section is essential for MOSA research because it:

1. Established the original definition of "modular open system approach"
2. Created the reporting requirement (MOSA or explain why not)
3. Linked MOSA to technical data rights (§809)
4. Set the January 1, 2019 effective date (Milestone A/B programs)
