# GAO Report Summary: DOD Needs Better Planning for Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA)

**Report:** GAO-25-106931 | **Date:** January 2025  
**Source:** [GAO-25-106931 PDF](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-25-106931.pdf)

## Executive Summary
The Department of Defense (DOD) is not effectively planning for or implementing a **Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA)** in weapon systems acquisition, limiting its ability to realize benefits like reduced sustainment costs and faster technology upgrades. While legislation requires MOSA implementation "to the maximum extent practicable," GAO found significant gaps in planning, resourcing, and policy.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. MOSA Implementation is Inconsistent and Not Informed by Cost-Benefit Analysis
*   **14 of 20** reviewed programs reported implementing MOSA to some extent.
*   **None of the 20 programs** conducted a formal cost-benefit analysis for MOSA, as DOD policy does not require one.
*   Decisions were often based on **near-term acquisition cost and schedule concerns**, overlooking long-term life-cycle benefits (sustainment costs are ~70% of total life-cycle cost).
*   **Example:** The Navy's AARGM-ER program decided against MOSA due to perceived costs, while the Air Force's SiAW program (modifying the same missile) was directed by leadership to implement MOSA.

### 2. Program and Portfolio Planning is Ineffective
*   Most programs **did not address all key MOSA planning elements** (e.g., identifying major system components, describing interfaces) in required acquisition documents.
*   Military departments did not effectively ensure programs addressed these elements.
*   **Only 2 of 9 Program Executive Offices (PEOs)** had a formal process to coordinate MOSA across their portfolio of programs, missing opportunities for cost savings and interoperability.

### 3. Military Departments Have Not Fully Identified or Prioritized MOSA Resources
*   Departments have not conducted enterprise-level assessments of MOSA resource needs (e.g., funding, expertise, digital tools).
*   **Key resource gaps include:**
    *   **Repositories** for storing shared MOSA interface files (development is in process but incomplete).
    *   **Enterprise licenses** for open standards (programs must obtain them individually).
    *   **MOSA subject matter experts** (funding requests have been rejected).
    *   **Digital engineering tools** (not universally available or funded).

### 4. Significant Gaps Remain in DOD Policy, Regulations, and Guidance
*   **MTA Pathway Gap:** DOD policy for the Middle Tier of Acquisition (MTA) pathway does not address MOSA requirements, despite the statutory mandate.
*   **Regulations Delayed:** Statutorily required regulations (from 2011, 2016, 2021 laws) on data rights and MOSA are still being drafted.
*   **Guidance Incomplete:** OSD has not issued required comprehensive MOSA guidance. Military department guidance is uneven:
    *   **Army:** Has detailed MOSA guidebook.
    *   **Air Force/Space Force:** Have guidebooks, but they don't cover all programs.
    *   **Navy:** Has not issued MOSA guidance.

## Key Quotes & Data Points

> "A MOSA enables weapon programs to better respond to changing threats by allowing them to replace components more easily."

> "Programs’ life-cycle outcomes—such as reduced sustainment costs and insertion of new technologies—are in part determined by decisions made early in the acquisition process."

*   **Sustainment costs** account for approximately **70 percent** of a weapon system’s total life-cycle cost.
*   **14 of 20** programs reported implementing MOSA.
*   **0 of 20** programs conducted a formal MOSA cost-benefit analysis.
*   **2 of 9** PEOs had a formal MOSA coordination process.

---

## Recommendations & DOD Response
GAO made **14 recommendations** to DOD, including:
1.  Develop a method for assessing MOSA costs and benefits.
2.  Improve processes to ensure programs address key MOSA planning elements.
3.  Establish formal processes for coordinating MOSA across program portfolios.
4.  Identify and prioritize MOSA resources at the department level.
5.  Issue MOSA policy for the MTA pathway and complete required guidance.

**DOD concurred with all 14 recommendations** and described planned actions. However, GAO noted DOD's response to the recommendation for Navy guidance may not fully address the statutory requirements.

---

## Background: What is MOSA?
*   **Definition:** An integrated business and technical strategy using modular design and open interfaces to allow components to be easily replaced or upgraded.
*   **Goal:** Increase competition, reduce sustainment costs, and enable rapid technology insertion.
*   **Legislative Mandate:**
    *   **FY2017 NDAA:** Required MOSA for Major Defense Acquisition Programs (MDAPs) entering development after Jan. 1, 2019.
    *   **FY2021 NDAA:** Expanded the requirement to **all** acquisition programs.
