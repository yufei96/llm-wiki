# Modernizing Military Simulation with Open Systems Architecture

**Source:** Breaking Defense | Sponsored by CAE Defense & Security
**Date:** February 2024

## Overview

Best-of-breed common models and architecture enable virtual training and testing with the same data in a cyber-resilient posture. This Q&A with CAE USA discusses the SCARS (Simulator Common Architecture Requirements and Standards) program.

## Key Participants

- **Christopher Marshall**, Lead Program Manager, CAE USA
- **Chris "SLAM" Duncan**, Director of SCARS Business Development, CAE USA

## Key Points

**Marshall on SCARS:**
> SCARS is a 10-year ID/IQ to develop a centralized enterprise solution that establishes a common open-systems architecture for U.S. Air Force (USAF) simulators, platforms, and the Joint Synthetic Environment.

**Marshall on the Vision:**
> The vision of SCARS is to create a cyber-resilient posture and provide the ability to utilize common models across all training systems – to train and test virtually, using the same data with an enterprise-level solution. The foundation of this vision is dependent on fully virtualizing the training systems onto a common hardware stack with a common architecture – the SCARS OPE.
