# Summary: Army's XM-30 Mechanized Infantry Combat Vehicle (Formerly OMFV)

## **Background & Purpose**
- **Program Name:** XM-30 Mechanized Infantry Combat Vehicle (redesignated from Optionally Manned Fighting Vehicle [OMFV] on June 26, 2023).
- **Mission:** To replace the aging M-2 Bradley Infantry Fighting Vehicle (in service since 1981), which has reached its technological limits.
- **Key Capability:** **Optionally manned** – can conduct remotely controlled operations without a crew onboard.
- **Role:** Part of an Armored Brigade Combat Team (ABCT), it maneuvers soldiers to a point of positional advantage to engage in close combat and deliver decisive lethality against near-peer competitors.
- **Predecessor Failures:** Two prior replacement programs (Future Combat System [FCS] and Ground Combat Vehicle [GCV]) were cancelled due to programmatic and cost issues.

## **Acquisition Approach & Design**
- **Acquisition Path:** **Middle Tier Acquisition Rapid Prototyping (MTA-RP)** program.
- **Design Philosophy:**
    - First Army ground vehicle designed using **state-of-the-art digital engineering tools**.
    - Built on a **Modular Open Systems Architecture (MOSA)** based on an Army-owned open standard.
    - MOSA is intended to facilitate rapid, cost-effective modernization and increase industry competition.

## **Five-Phase Acquisition Plan**
1.  **Phase 1:** Market Research and Requirement Development.
2.  **Phase 2:** Concept Design (Modeling, Simulation, and Analysis).
3.  **Phase 3:** Detailed Design (culminates in Critical Design Review [CDR]).
4.  **Phase 4:** Prototype Build and Test (includes Limited User Test [LUT]).
5.  **Phase 5:** Production and Fielding (Low-Rate Initial Production [LRIP]).

## **Program Timeline & Key Milestones**
- **July 2021:** Five firms awarded Phase 2 contracts (~$299.4M total) for concept design.
- **June 2023:** Two firms awarded Phase 3 & 4 contracts (~$1.6B total) for detailed design and prototyping:
    - General Dynamics Land Systems Inc.
    - American Rheinmetall Vehicles LLC
- **August 2024:** Both competitors completed Preliminary Design Review (PDR).
- **June 2025:** **Milestone B approved**, advancing the program into engineering and manufacturing development.
- **February 2026:** **Milestone B decision paused**. Army leadership did not sign final documentation to avoid locking into a specific design and to allow for a potential major reworking of the program.
- **FY2028 (Q1):** Target for **Milestone C** (production decision).
- **FY2029:** Expected first vehicle fielding.

## **Recent Developments (2026)**
- **Program Reevaluation:** The pause in Milestone B was to "leave the door open to a major reworking" of the program.
- **Army Statement on Competition:**
    > "We are actively assessing multiple, competing designs for the XM-30 to foster a truly competitive environment. We continue to look for partners who can deliver cutting-edge solutions now, not decades from now."
- **FY2027 Budget Request:** $547 million in procurement funding for **19 prototype vehicles** for evaluation. Prime contractor(s) have not been identified.
- **Fall 2026 Testing:** The **1st Cavalry Division** (Fort Hood, TX) will be the first unit to receive prototypes. Two platoons of XM-30s and one platoon of M-1E3 Abrams tanks will undergo operational testing at the National Training Center (NTC) through March 2027.

## **Potential Congressional Oversight Issue**
- **Organic Counter-UAS (C-UAS) Capability:** While the XM-30 will have an active protection system (APS) for anti-tank threats, it is **unclear if it will have an integrated C-UAS capability**.
- **Debate:** Some argue that drones and loitering munitions could make armored vehicles obsolete; others believe new technologies are needed to improve survivability against aerial threats.
- **Congressional Action:** Congress may examine the Army's approach to protecting the XM-30 from aerial threats.
