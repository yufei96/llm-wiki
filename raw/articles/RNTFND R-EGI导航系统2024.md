# The future of navigation (MOSA) is happening! - USAF's R-EGI Project

## Core Development
The U.S. Air Force is advancing its **Modular Open Systems Architecture (MOSA)** approach for navigation through the **R-EGI (Resilient-Embedded GPS/INS)** program.

## Key Problem & MOSA Solution
*   **Traditional Challenge:** Integrating or upgrading navigation systems (like GPS) into aircraft or weapons often requires significant disassembly and rebuilding of the platform, creating a **"huge barrier to making upgrades."**
*   **MOSA Advantage:** This architecture makes it **"much easier to add and subtract navigation sensors and capabilities,"** moving toward a more plug-and-play model for future navigation signals and sensors.

## Important Details & Context
*   **R-EGI Definition:** Stands for **Resilient-Embedded GPS/INS**.
*   **Legacy Platform Solution:** For older systems with deeply embedded GPS/GNSS, a proposed solution involves a multi-navigation package that ingests various signals, translates them into a GPS/GNSS signal, and **feeds it to the legacy platform through its existing GPS antenna port**. This concept was presented by Nino De Falcis of VIAVI Solutions at CGSIC.
    *   **Presentation Link:** [CGSIC Presentation by Nino De Falcis](https://www.gps.gov/cgsic/meetings/2024/defalcis.pdf)
*   **Related Milestone:** The R-EGI program has achieved a key milestone, as reported by *Inside GNSS*.
    *   **Article Link:** [R-EGI Program Achieves Key Milestone](https://insidegnss.com/r-egi-program-achieves-key-milestone/)
