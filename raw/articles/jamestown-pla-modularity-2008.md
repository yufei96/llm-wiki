# The PLA's Evolving Operational Doctrine: Experiments in Modularity

来源：Jamestown Foundation, China Brief, Volume 8 Issue 5
URL: https://jamestown.org/the-plas-evolving-operational-doctrine-experiments-in-modularity/
抓取日期: 2026-05-07

## Overview

The People's Liberation Army (PLA) is transforming into a **Hardened and Networked Army (HNA)** by creating modular, combined-arms forces optimized for complex, urbanized, and high-altitude warfare. This shift emphasizes information operations, network-enabled capabilities, and rapid deployability.

## Key Concepts

- **"Complex Warfighting"**: Core doctrine identifying modern battlefield as complex, diverse, diffuse, and highly lethal. Forces must perform wide range of tasks simultaneously in urbanized terrain.
- **Modular Combined Arms Battle Groups**: Fundamental organizational unit. Semi-autonomous, highly networked teams (squad to battalion size) capable of independent operations within a joint framework.
- **Pei Shu (Attaching Troops)**: Creating independent battle groups within a division or augmenting a division with heavier forces for specific operations.
- **Zhi Chi (To Support)**: Establishing battlefield logistics organization to support forces operating deep behind enemy lines.

## Organizational Changes

- Shift from Divisions to Brigades/Battalions: Three-level command structure of corps, brigade, and battalion.
- Enhanced Command & Control: Unit headquarters deployed as battle group headquarters.
- Increased Firepower & Mobility: Focus on protected mobility, network capability, and combat readiness.

## Strategic Deployment

- Primary theaters: Xinjiang and Tibet
- Out-of-area capability: Sustained combined-arms operations deep beyond China's frontiers

## 分析点评

此文2008年发表，显示PLA的模块化改革早于美军将MOSA写入法律（2017年NDAA），但性质不同——PLA的"模块化"是部队编制层面的合成化改革，而非武器系统层面的开放架构。两者虽然共享"模块化"术语，但属于不同层面的实践。
