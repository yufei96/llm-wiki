## Overview

The **LGM-35 Sentinel** is a next-generation intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) being developed to replace the aging LGM-30 Minuteman III as the land-based leg of the United States nuclear triad.

## Background

The LGM-35 Sentinel (formerly known as the Ground Based Strategic Deterrent, or GBSD) is designed to ensure the continued viability of the U.S. nuclear deterrent force well into the 21st century. The Minuteman III, which has been in service since 1970, requires modernization to address evolving threats and technological challenges.

## Development

### Program History
- **2010:** The program gained momentum as part of an agreement to support the New START treaty ratification.
- **2017:** Initial development contracts awarded — $329 million to Northrop Grumman and $349 million to Boeing.
- **2019:** Boeing withdrew from the competition, making Northrop Grumman the sole prime contractor.
- **2022 (April):** The system was officially designated **LGM-35A Sentinel**.

### Designation
The "LGM" designation stands for:
- **L** = Silo-launched
- **G** = Surface attack
- **M** = Guided missile

### Design Philosophy
The Sentinel emphasizes:
- **Digital engineering technologies**
- **Modular open system architecture**
- Easier maintenance to reduce lifecycle costs
- Long-term relevance and adaptability

## Arsenal Scale
Upon completion, the system will comprise:
- **400 missiles**
- **450 missile silos**
- **600 ICBM facilities**
- Spread across over **40,000 square miles** of U.S. territory

## Timeline & Service
- **Entry into Service:** The Sentinel is set to enter Department of Defense service by **2029 or 2030** for integrated launch and support systems.
- **Operational Lifespan:** The system is commissioned to remain in service until **2075**.

## Warheads
- **W87 mod 0** thermonuclear warhead (300 kilotons of TNT)
- **W87 mod 1** thermonuclear warhead (475 kilotons of TNT)
- Deployment targeted for 2028, with the W87-0 as a backup
- **Mk21A** re-entry vehicle proposed to launch the warhead

## Strategic Significance
The Sentinel program is critical for maintaining U.S. nuclear security dominance. It ensures the continued viability of the ground-based deterrent leg as other legs of the triad (submarines and bombers) also undergo upgrades. The program's focus on cost-effectiveness and modern engineering aims to deliver a reliable and adaptable system for decades to come.

## Contractors
- **Prime Contractor:** Northrop Grumman
- **Subcontractors:** Network of subcontractors supporting various program elements
