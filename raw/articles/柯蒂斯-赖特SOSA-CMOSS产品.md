# Summary: MOSA, SOSA, and CMOSS in Defense

## Core Concepts

### Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA)
*   **Definition:** An **acquisition and design strategy**, not a technical standard. It prioritizes the use of open standards-based technology to create "affordable and adaptable" systems.
*   **Legal Mandate:** Codified into U.S. law (**Title 10 U.S.C. 2446a.(b), Sec 805**), requiring all new defense acquisition programs to be designed and developed using MOSA.
*   **Strategic Imperative:** A 2019 U.S. DoD memorandum declared MOSA a "Warfighting Imperative," mandating that MOSA-supporting standards be included in requirements for all future weapons systems. This was driven by the need to rapidly integrate new technologies to counter evolving threats.

### Key MOSA-Supporting Standards
The DoD directive accelerated the adoption of specific open standards, primarily:
*   **CMOSS (Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Cyber, Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (C4ISR) Modular Open Suite of Standards)**
*   **SOSA (Sensor Open Systems Architecture)**

### SOSA Technical Standard
*   **Purpose:** Combines MOSA principles with security to enable the rapid development and deployment of secure sensor processing systems.
*   **Focus:** Provides a framework for designing and building sensor systems using open standards, enhancing interoperability and technology refresh.

## Key Quotes & Facts

> "A Modular Open Systems Approach (MOSA) is not itself a technical standard. Rather, MOSA is an acquisition and design strategy that prioritizes the use of open standards-based technology for... 'designing an affordable and adaptable system.'"

> "The memo mandates that MOSA supporting standards should be included in requirements for all weapons systems going forward."

*   **Legal Reference:** Title 10 U.S.C. 2446a.(b), Sec 805

## Context & Driver
The U.S. DoD and global defense forces are adopting MOSA to address rapidly evolving threats. An open standards-based architecture allows for:
*   Quick integration and deployment of new battlefield functionality.
*   Effective information sharing.
*   Enhanced communication within and between vehicles.
