# Air Force Integrates A-GRA Across Multiple CCA Platforms

## Overview
The U.S. Air Force is actively integrating and testing its **Autonomy Government Reference Architecture (A-GRA)** across multiple vendor platforms as part of the **Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCA)** program. This effort supports the National Defense Strategy and is a key step in the service's acquisition reform strategy to advance modular open systems, promote competition, and accelerate capability delivery.

## Key Developments
*   **Integration & Testing:** Mission autonomy vendors **Collins Aerospace (RTX subsidiary)** and **Shield AI** are integrating A-GRA and have begun semi-autonomous flight testing.
*   **Platforms:** Testing is occurring on the **YFQ-42** (General Atomics) and **YFQ-44** (Anduril) CCA platforms.
*   **Strategic Goal:** To prevent "vendor lock" and create a competitive ecosystem for autonomy algorithms.

## What is A-GRA?
A-GRA is a **government-owned modular open systems framework** designed to establish a universal standard for mission autonomy across platforms.

> **"Verifying A-GRA across multiple partners is critical to our acquisition strategy. It proves that we are not locked into a single solution or a single vendor. We are instead building a competitive ecosystem where the best algorithms can be deployed rapidly to the warfighter on any A-GRA compliant platform, regardless of the vendor providing the algorithm."**
> — **Col. Timothy Helfrich**, Portfolio Acquisition Executive for Fighters and Advanced Aircraft

According to the Agile Development Office director, integrating A-GRA onto multiple platforms quickly demonstrates the open-systems approach is working and enables accelerated iteration of tactics and capabilities across the fleet.

## What is the Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCA) Program?
The CCA is the Air Force's initiative to develop **semi-autonomous aircraft** designed to operate alongside crewed platforms as force multipliers. It is part of the **Next Generation Air Dominance Family of Systems** and seeks to integrate open-systems architectures for continuous iteration of autonomy and mission systems.

### CCA Program Milestones:
*   **April 2024:** Air Force selected **General Atomics** and **Anduril** to build production-representative prototypes.
*   **August 2025:** The **YFQ-42A prototype** (General Atomics) completed its inaugural flight and transitioned into flight testing.
