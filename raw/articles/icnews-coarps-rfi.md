# Air Force Posts COARPS RFI Summary

## Key RFI Details
- **Issuing Agency:** U.S. Air Force
- **RFI Title:** AWACS Common Open Architecture Radar Programs (COARPS)
- **Issuance Date:** June 2
- **Response Deadline:** July 16, 4:00 p.m. Eastern
- **Full Information Link:** [SAM.gov Opportunity](https://sam.gov/opp/48da7c685db04ea394080bfc784d9b68/view)

## Purpose & Objective
The RFI aims to **identify industry partners** developing and/or integrating COARPS modes into their radar programs. The Air Force's adoption of COARPS is a key step toward open architecture, which promises to:
- Democratize radar development
- Foster competition
- Drive innovation
- Reduce cost and integration time

## Background & Context
### Advanced Battlefield Management System (ABMS)
- Envisioned as a **decentralized system-of-systems**.
- Draws information from multiple sensors for **real-time situational awareness and decision making**.
- Radar mission systems are a key piece of its architecture.

### COARPS Specification
COARPS is an open radar architecture specification developed to enable:
- Radar subsystem procurement from independent sources.
- Processor hardware refresh without major software rewrites.
- Insertion of third-party mode technology.
- Reduction of radar mode acquisition timelines.

### Recent Air Force Initiatives
- In 2019, the Air Force started several **ABMS GMTI (Ground Moving Target Indicator) programs** focusing on COARPS-compliant GMTI mode development.
- Goals included path-finding third-party mode development, evolving the COARPS standard, building transferable GMTI modes, and establishing an AF radar mode repository.
- As these programs conclude, the Air Force recognizes more work has been done on COARPS compliance.
