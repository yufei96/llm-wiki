## Modular Open Systems Approaches (MOSA): Empowering DoD's IP Cadre  
**Source:** Public Contract Law Journal, Vol. 54, No. 1 (Fall 2024)  
**Authors:** Sean Bahram Zehtab & Thomas S Hong (US Army JAG Corps)  

---

### **Key Excerpts**  
> **Gen. Mark Milley (2021):**  
> *"Utilizing cloud-based networks and agile software development, our modular open sourced systems will deliver the necessary strategic operational advantage required within both competition and conflict."*  

> **Military Service Secretaries (2019):**  
> *"Victory... will be in part determined by our ability to rapidly share information across domains."*  

> **Statutory Definition (10 U.S.C. § 4401(b)(4)):**  
> *"A modular system interface is a shared boundary between major systems, major system components, or modular systems, defined by various physical, logical, and functional characteristics."*  

> **DFARS 252.227-7013(a)(13) - Government Purpose Rights:**  
> *"Rights to use, modify, reproduce, release, perform, display, or disclose technical data within the Government without restriction."*

### **Summary of Key Information**  

#### **I. MOSA Fundamentals & Strategic Importance**  
- **Definition:** Systems with "highly cohesive, loosely coupled, and severable modules" using open interfaces (Defense Acquisition University).  
- **Benefits:**  
  - Enables competition for subsystem upgrades (e.g., aircraft radar).  
  - Reduces "vendor lock" and lifecycle costs.  
  - Supports interoperability for multi-domain operations (JADC2).  
- **National Security Imperative:** Critical for "recomposable kill chains" – decentralized systems that adapt if nodes are destroyed.  

#### **II. Legislative Evolution (FY2017–FY2024 NDAAs)**  
- **FY2017 NDAA §805:** Mandated MOSA for Major Defense Acquisition Programs (MDAPs).  
- **FY2018 NDAA §802:** Created DoD IP Cadre (10 U.S.C. § 3791) and required IP policies.  
- **FY2021 NDAA §804:**  
  - Expanded MOSA requirement to non-MDAPs "to the maximum extent practicable."  
  - Granted DoD **government purpose rights** for modular interfaces.  
- **FY2023 NDAA §841:** Directed IP Cadre to develop MOSA case studies and guidelines.  
- **Congressional Dissatisfaction:** Repeated mandates reflect frustration with slow MOSA adoption.  

#### **III. Implementation Challenges**  
1. **Industry Business Model:**  
   - Prime contractors (consolidated to 5 major firms) resist open systems to protect revenue.  
   - Counterpoint: MOSA may increase opportunities for smaller firms (BAE Systems).  
2. **Workforce Gaps:**  
   - GAO (2013, 2021): DoD lacks MOSA/IP expertise and training metrics.  
   - DAU workshops exist but lack advanced simulations.  
3. **Acquisition Process:**  
   - Interfaces defined too late (post-Milestone B).  
   - **Standard Conflict:** Statutory "maximum extent practicable" vs. DoD Instruction 5010.44’s "cost-effective and feasible."  

#### **IV. MOSA Successes & Standards**  
- **Virginia-Class Submarines:** MOSA-based command systems (CSRR) and sonar arrays.  
- **Open Standards:**  
  - **FACE:** Avionics software standard with "app store" model.  
  - **SOSA/CMOSS:** Sensor and electronic warfare standards.  
  - **DARPA STITCHES:** Integrates legacy systems without hardware upgrades.  

#### **V. IP Cadre’s Proposed Role**  
- **Current State:** Small OSD office (5 staff) + federated experts from services.  
- **Recommendations:**  
  1. Develop **evaluation criteria** for IP/MOSA effectiveness (technical, business, sustainment).  
  2. **Collect and assess** program IP strategies (pilot program).  
  3. **Legislative Action:** Amend 10 U.S.C. §§ 1707/3791 to mandate reviews.  
- **Evaluation Framework (Figures 2–3):**  
  - Technical feasibility, interface conformance, sustainment cost reduction.  

#### **VI. Conclusion & Recommendations**  
- **MOSA is a national security priority** for modernizing the $2T+ force.  
- **Critical Actions:**  
  - Empower IP Cadre to create MOSA evaluation metrics.  
  - Launch pilot program to review select programs.  
  - Resolve "maximum extent practicable" vs. "cost-effective" standard conflict.
