# LGM-35A Sentinel: Breaking Down the Basics of Ground-Based Strategic Deterrent

## Overview
The **LGM-35A Sentinel** (formerly Ground-Based Strategic Deterrent or GBSD) is the next-generation intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) designed to replace the aging **LGM-30 Minuteman III**. It represents the modernized land-based leg of the U.S. nuclear triad, crucial for maintaining global nuclear deterrence against modernizing adversaries.

## Key Features & Specifications
*   **Primary Mission:** Nuclear deterrence as part of the U.S. nuclear triad (land, sea, air).
*   **Warhead:** Equipped with **W87-1 thermonuclear warheads**, replacing the Minuteman III's W78 warheads. Deployment is targeted for 2028, with the **W87-0** as a backup.
*   **Re-entry Vehicle:** The **Mk21A** is proposed to launch the warhead.
*   **Design Philosophy:** Emphasizes **digital engineering technologies**, a **modular open system architecture**, and easier maintenance to reduce lifecycle costs and ensure long-term relevance.
*   **Arsenal Scale:** Upon completion, the system will comprise **400 missiles**, **450 missile silos**, and **600 ICBM facilities** across over 40,000 square miles of U.S. territory.

## Development & Contractors
*   **Primary Contractor:** **Northrop Grumman** became the sole prime contractor after Boeing withdrew from the competition in July 2019.
*   **Initial Awards:** In 2017, Northrop Grumman received $329 million and Boeing $349 million for initial development contracts.
*   **Subcontractors:** Northrop Grumman works with a network of subcontractors for the program.
*   **Official Designation:** The system was officially designated **LGM-35A Sentinel** in April 2022. The "LGM" designation stands for **L** (silo-launched), **G** (surface attack), **M** (guided missile).

## Timeline & Service
*   **Predecessor:** The Minuteman III has been in service since 1970.
*   **Modernization Push:** The program gained momentum in 2010 as part of an agreement to support the New START treaty ratification.
*   **Entry into Service:** The Sentinel is set to enter Department of Defense service by **2029 or 2030** for integrated launch and support systems.
*   **Operational Lifespan:** The system is commissioned to remain in service until **2075**.

## Strategic Significance
The Sentinel program is critical for maintaining U.S. nuclear security dominance. It ensures the continued viability of the ground-based deterrent leg as other legs of the triad (submarines and bombers) also undergo upgrades. The program's focus on cost-effectiveness and modern engineering aims to deliver a reliable and adaptable system for decades to come.
